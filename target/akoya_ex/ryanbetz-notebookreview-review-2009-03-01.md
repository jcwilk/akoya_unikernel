## Product context

- Part of a North American Medion lineup: Akoya LS (12.1"), **Akoya EX (14.1")**, Akoya XL (15.4").
- Marketed as a portable multimedia notebook; purchased at Best Buy (reviewer's configuration).

## Observed on tested unit (additional facts)

- Chassis: white plastic; battery protrudes at rear.
- Display: 16 brightness levels; DVI-D and VGA external video outputs confirmed.
- CPU codename referenced in benchmarks: Pentium M **Dothan**.
- Storage benchmark (HD Tune): drive model **SAMSUNG MP0804H** in the reviewed unit.
- No Bluetooth; IrDA present for device sync.
- Wi-Fi toggled via Fn+F1 (no mechanical Wi-Fi switch).

## I/O ports

- PCMCIA Type II slot
- 3× USB 2.0 (2 rear, 1 left)
- VGA out
- DVI out (DVI-D dual link)
- IrDA (infrared)
- IEEE 1394 (FireWire)
- 10/100 Mbit Fast Ethernet
- 56k V.90 modem
- Microphone input
- Line out with S/PDIF

## Software / OS (as shipped on reviewed unit)

- Microsoft Windows XP Home Edition SP2
- Microsoft Works 8.0
- CyberLink PowerCinema Suite
- Ahead Nero Burning ROM with Nero Recode 2-SE
- Norton AntiVirus (90-day updates)
- 6 GB recovery partition (D: drive) for factory restore

## Performance notes (reviewer-measured, same unit)

- Super Pi (2M digits): 1m 59s
- 3DMark 2001 SE: 2500
- 3DMark 2003 v3.50: 118
- Battery (wireless on, ~600 MHz, low brightness): ~5 hours
- Battery (full load / games / movies): ~2.5–3 hours
